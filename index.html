About Us

The tale of the legendary University House (UH) dates back to 1919 when it was opened in Gardens, near the Hiddingh Campus. Its name was bestowed upon it by the original warden Professor Lawrence Crawford. Over the years UH has proved to resilient despite a number of temporary closures and relocations. UH no longer resides in this location - as it was closed in 1937 and is now the site of Gardens Commercial High School. The residence was then moved to lower campus where it was again eventually closed to accomodate the opening of the UCT Graduate School of Business. After such a storied past it is now located (as it has been since 1958) on 1 Matopo Road in a structure originally built as a temporary housing for WWII servicemen in 1946. The location on Matopo Road was originally intended to house graduate and more mature students but is now of of UCT's first teir residences. University House’s four blocks house 84 males in single rooms and is within easy walking distance of Upper Campus.

PHOTOS OF UNIVERSITY HOUSE

Living at University House is a once in a lifetime experience. It’s not just a place to stay during your studies. It’s a chance to meet new people and forge friendships, step out of your comfort zone, and grow as a person. UH has a uniquely strong sense of brotherhood owing to our relatively small size. This brotherhood brings with it a sense of belonging and community where everyone knows their neighbours with many even becoming friends.
